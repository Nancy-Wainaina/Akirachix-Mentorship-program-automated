Born A Crime by Trevor Noah

These two chapters dive into the life of a mixed-race child growing up during apartheid in South Africa raised by a strict Christian mother.
Trevor narrates the story of his mother’s unwavering faith and the challenges they faced due to the racial segregation Imposed by apartheid. In
this chapter, it highlights the tribal conflicts between the Zulu and Xhosa tribes.

Being raised in a Christian household,
Trevor is still not convinced that they should go to church every Sunday.
On the other hand, Trevor’s mother is governed by church and she wouldn't be convinced by any of Trevor’s excuses not to go to church.
Trevor’s mother is determined to keep her son safe even if it's risking her life to save him from a gang attack.

Starts with apartheid and its oppressive laws. Trevor was born to a white father and a black mother, and that union was illegal at that time.
This chapter shows the cruelty of apartheid as Trevor’s existence was considered a crime.
Connecting these issues to my county Nairobi, It's clear that discrimination is still taking place, where people coming from humble backgrounds are not
given jobs in high-class workplaces. Some are even considered outcasts. The theme of resilience in the face of misfortune is universal. In Nairobi,
we also have a division of churches, where some are considered for the rich and others considered for the poor.
